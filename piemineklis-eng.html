  In my vision, without regard to the real nature of it, I see it as a milestone, as “I'll be in five minutes.” It's always a relief to see that I've managed and the whole trip has been successful. It's not finished yet, but it'll be over now. If it is noted that the road has been long, much standing, it is so pleasing to be aware that it will soon be possible to get out of transport and walk. In the mornings, it is still interesting to see the crowds of people running past it, each in their own direction. The view alone gives an energy rush and a productivity-friendly mood. The monument itself seems aesthetically appealing to me, but I always have to look at it from Brīvibas Street, even though the most beautiful side of it is directed at Old Riga. The memory of the Freedom Monument always comes to mind either the rows of flowers on November 18 or those soldiers who walk past it in deep peace. And now, by thinking about those moments, it is time to step out of transport and go toward university.
