CHURCH

  When travelling to a university with public transport, this is my choice to step outside if it is impossible to get closer. I'm always amazed by those people who are doing something in that church in the early morning, but even more I notice those people around it. It is a huge social diversification – tourists walk past homeless people, people donate money to disabled people, people of one religion meet or at least look at others.

  It's like a palette of colors for Purvitis's painting, which doesn't mix, but it's there. In addition, architecture raises thoughts about how much time and attention has been focused on making it look the way it looks. In my case, I get past several, but of course the gilded domes are paying attention, which in sunny days makes you want to wear sunglasses. In addition, it is sometimes pleasing to think that I also have faith in the way I passed them, that the university is the church in which I can gain faith in myself and my own abilities. But at some point the door will open and I'll have another focus.
